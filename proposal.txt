Project Overview:
My project will create a language evolution simulation, which will be designed
to simulate how concepts and words evolve in English over the span of 25
generations. This program will aim to demonstrate some possible sociolinguistic
alterations to common words or phrases in a way that mimics real-life language change.

Implementation:
I plan to use a std::map to store and manage a corpus of linguistic data relating
to common word concepts (hello, goodbye, etc.) of American English. The map will
categorize these concepts into three std::lists representing slang, casual, and
formal speech. For example, for the concept 'hello,' examples of each would be
'sup'(slang), 'hi'(casual), and 'greetings'(formal). The aim would be to have about
3-4 words per list per concept. The map's key will be the concept ('hello' in this
example), and the map's value will be an array of these three lists.

Simulated Events:
The simulation will focus on four events: words moving from an informal tier to
the next level of formality (i.e. slang becoming casual or casual words becoming
formal), word death/extinction (words falling out of use), new slang being created, 
and revival of formal vernacular back into slang (i.e. 'groovy' or 'gnarly' making 
a comeback). During any given generation, any of these events has a chance to occur.
